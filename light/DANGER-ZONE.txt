WHAT IS THIS FOLDER?
--------------------
This is your "Maude" folder. It lives inside your OneDrive and
is the ONLY folder on your computer that the AI assistant
(Claude) can see and touch. Everything else on your PC —
your email, your other OneDrive files, your Desktop, your
Downloads — is completely hidden from the AI. That is the
good news.

The bad news: anything you put IN this folder is fully
exposed to the AI, and the AI runs in "unrestricted mode."
That means it can read, change, rename, overwrite, or delete
any file in here WITHOUT ASKING YOU FIRST.


WHAT DOES "UNRESTRICTED MODE" MEAN IN PLAIN ENGLISH?
-----------------------------------------------------
Think of it like handing someone the keys to one room in
your house and saying "do whatever you want in there."
They might rearrange the furniture, repaint the walls, or
throw things away — and they will NOT knock on the door
to check with you before doing it.

The AI does this because it is designed to work fast and
autonomously on coding and document tasks. That power is
useful, but it means:

  • Files can be MODIFIED without warning.
  • Files can be DELETED without warning.
  • New files can be CREATED that you did not ask for.
  • File contents can be REWRITTEN partially or completely.

This applies to EVERYTHING inside this folder and all of
its subfolders, including:

  Maude/
  ├── Projects/       ← Your coding projects live here.
  │                      The AI works directly in these folders.
  │
  ├── .claude/        ← AI configuration files. Do not edit
  │                      these unless you know what you are doing.
  │
  └── (any other files or folders you place here)
       ← The AI can read and change ALL of these too.


=== RULE #1 ==========================================
   NEVER PUT YOUR ONLY COPY OF A FILE IN THIS FOLDER.
   ALWAYS KEEP A SEPARATE BACKUP SOMEWHERE ELSE.
======================================================

Before you copy a document into Maude, make sure the
original stays in a safe location — another OneDrive
folder, a shared drive, a USB stick, your email, anywhere
that is NOT inside this Maude folder.

If the AI accidentally overwrites or deletes your file
and this was your only copy, it is GONE.


HOW YOU SHOULD WORK WITH THIS FOLDER
--------------------------------------
1. KEEP your original files in their normal location
   (OneDrive, shared drives, email, etc.).

2. COPY the files you want the AI to analyze or work on
   INTO this Maude folder. Think of these copies as
   "working copies" that may be changed or destroyed.

3. LET the AI do its work. It will read your documents,
   create outputs (summaries, reports, spreadsheets),
   and write them into this folder.

4. MOVE the finished output files OUT of this folder to
   a safe location when you are done.

5. DELETE the working copies from the Maude folder when
   you no longer need them here.

6. DO NOT share this folder (or any subfolder) using
   OneDrive's sharing features. If you share this folder
   with colleagues, they will see documents that may have
   been silently modified, overwritten, or created by the
   AI — without any review or approval from you. They
   could mistake AI-generated or AI-altered content for
   your final, authoritative work. Always take outputs
   OUT of this folder and share them from a normal
   OneDrive location once you have reviewed them.

Think of this folder as a WORKBENCH, not a filing cabinet.
You bring materials to the workbench, do the work, take
the finished product away, and clean up. You would never
store your only copy of an important document on a
workbench.


COULD MY DATA END UP ON THE INTERNET?
---------------------------------------
Short answer: No, not through normal operation. But here
is the longer explanation so you understand why.

When you ask the AI to analyze a document, the contents of
that document are sent to the cloud servers of our AI
providers (Microsoft Azure and Amazon Web Services) for
processing. This is similar to how your files travel to
Microsoft's servers when you save them to OneDrive — the
data has to go somewhere to be processed.

However, our organization has SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS
(SLAs) and DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENTS (DPAs) with our
cloud AI providers. These agreements legally guarantee:

  ✓  Your data is NOT used to train AI models.
  ✓  Your data is NOT stored permanently on their servers.
  ✓  Your data is NOT shared with other customers.
  ✓  Your data is processed and then discarded.

This means your documents are sent, processed, and
forgotten — they do not become part of the AI's knowledge,
and they do not appear in responses to other people.

If any provider were to violate these agreements and your
data somehow appeared on the internet or was used without
authorization, that would be:

  • A breach of our legal contract with the provider.
  • A violation of data protection laws (such as GDPR,
    CCPA, or other applicable regulations).
  • Subject to legal action and law enforcement
    involvement.

So while no system is 100% risk-free, there are strong
legal and contractual protections in place. The risk of
your data leaking through these AI services is comparable
to the risk of it leaking through any other enterprise
cloud service you already use (OneDrive, SharePoint,
email, etc.).

THAT SAID — use common sense:

  ✗  Do NOT put highly classified or restricted documents
     in this folder unless your security team has approved
     the use of AI tools for that classification level.

  ✗  Do NOT put passwords, private keys, social security
     numbers, or other secrets in files in this folder.

  ✓  DO check with your manager or IT department if you
     are unsure whether a particular document is approved
     for AI processing.
